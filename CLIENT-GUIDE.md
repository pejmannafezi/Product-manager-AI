# AI PM Command Center — A Simple Guide

Welcome! This guide explains, in plain language, what this app does and how each
part helps you. No technical knowledge needed.

## What is this app, in one sentence?

It's a single place to **plan, run, and govern your AI projects** — especially in
sensitive fields like healthcare — so nothing important slips through the cracks.
Think of it as a smart project office that also knows the rules and best practices
and helps you follow them.

## How it helps you (the big picture)

- It keeps **all your project work in one place** — tasks, plans, risks, decisions.
- It has **built-in expert knowledge** (a 23-chapter guide) you can search anytime.
- It **checks your project against good practice** before you start, so you fix
  gaps early instead of discovering them too late.
- It can use **AI to give smarter suggestions** — but it works perfectly fine even
  without AI turned on.
- It speaks **English and Persian** (with right-to-left layout), and works on
  desktop and phone.

# What each part does

You move between parts using the menu at the top of the screen.

## 🧭 Dashboard — your daily home base
**What it's for:** A quick "morning coffee" overview of everything that needs you.

**What you'll see:** Your top 3 priorities, anything overdue, open problems
(blockers), high risks, decisions waiting on you, and today's checklist — plus a
live feed of what the app has done for you.

**Why it helps:** You start each day knowing exactly what matters, without digging.

## 📋 Projects — the home for each piece of work
**What it's for:** Every project lives here. Open one to see everything about it.

Inside a project you get tabs for:
- **Tasks** — the to-do list for that project.
- **Plan** — the milestones (big checkpoints), deliverables (what you'll produce),
  recommended team, and risks.
- **Timeline** — a visual schedule (a "Gantt chart") showing what happens when, and
  which steps are most time-critical.
- **Roadmap** — what you're doing *now*, *next*, and *later*.
- **Risks, Decisions, Blockers** — track what could go wrong, choices you've made,
  and anything stuck.
- **Compliance** — how well the project meets good-practice standards.
- **Documents & Activity** — your files and a history of everything that happened.

**Why it helps:** Everything about a project is in one organized place — no more
scattered spreadsheets and documents.

## ✅ Tasks — your to-do list
**What it's for:** See and manage your action items. Tick them off as you go.

**Why it helps:** Clear, simple progress tracking.

## ☑️ Checklists — daily, weekly, and monthly routines
**What it's for:** Ready-made routines so you never forget recurring good habits
(like reviewing priorities each day or risks each week). Anything you don't finish
carries over to the next day automatically.

**Why it helps:** Builds discipline and consistency without extra effort.

## 📚 Knowledge — your built-in expert library
**What it's for:** A full 23-chapter guide on managing AI products, fully
searchable. Type a question and get the most relevant sections, with the matching
words highlighted.

**Why it helps:** Expert guidance is always a search away — like having a mentor
on call.

## 📖 References — add your own documents for the app to use
**What it's for:** Upload or paste your own materials (policies, templates, past
documents). The app will use them when giving you suggestions. You choose whether
a document applies to **all** projects or just **one**.

**Why it helps:** The app's advice becomes tailored to *your* organization's rules
and standards.

## 🔍 Glossary — plain-language definitions
**What it's for:** Quick definitions of AI and product terms.

**Why it helps:** No more getting lost in jargon.

## 🛡️ Compliance — a readiness check before you start
**What it's for:** Upload a project document and the app reviews it against a
governance checklist. You get a **score and grade**, a list of what's missing,
and recommendations — each pointing to the exact guide chapter that explains it.

**Why it helps:** You catch problems *before* the project starts, when they're
cheap and easy to fix.

## 🔥 Issues — a guided way to solve problems
**What it's for:** Log a problem and the app helps you handle it properly. It rates
how urgent it is, and for serious ones it automatically creates the right tasks. It
also gives you a checklist you must complete before a problem can be marked
"closed," so nothing is left half-fixed.

**Why it helps:** Problems get solved thoroughly and on time — not forgotten.

## ➕ New Project Intake — the magic "start a project" button
**What it's for:** Tell the app about a new project (and optionally upload a
document), and it does the heavy lifting for you in one go:
1. Reviews it for compliance and readiness.
2. Suggests relevant reading from the guide.
3. Turns any gaps into a prioritized to-do list with due dates.
4. Builds a roadmap, a full plan with milestones and dates, and a risk list.
5. Sets up your daily/weekly/monthly checklists.
6. Writes a starting strategy outline.

**Why it helps:** What might take days of setup happens in moments, and you start
every project on solid footing.

## Good to know

- **Works without AI.** The app is fully usable on its own. If you turn on the AI
  option, suggestions get smarter — and you'll see an "AI-enhanced" badge where it
  was used. If AI is ever unavailable, the app simply falls back to its built-in
  logic; nothing breaks.
- **Two languages.** Switch between English and Persian anytime with the button in
  the top bar.
- **Light and dark mode.** It automatically matches your device's appearance.
- **Works on your phone.** The menu collapses into a tidy ☰ button on small screens.
- **Privacy reminder.** If you share the app with a team online, don't upload real
  patient or confidential data to a shared demo.

## Getting started in 3 steps

1. Open **New Project Intake** and describe your project (upload a document if you
   have one).
2. Review what the app generated — your compliance check, plan, roadmap, tasks, and
   reading list.
3. Use the **Dashboard** each day to stay on top of priorities, and tick off your
   **Checklists**.

That's it — you're running a well-governed AI project.
